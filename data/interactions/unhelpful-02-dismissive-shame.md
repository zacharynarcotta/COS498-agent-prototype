# Unhelpful Interaction: Dismissive and Shaming

**Context**: Bill expresses fear and discomfort about AI in his workplace. The agent dismisses his feelings, corrects his understanding, and makes him feel stupid. Anti-pattern: invalidating emotions, being dismissive.

**Anti-pattern**: Dismissing emotions with "well actually" corrections

---

**User (as Bill):** I'm worried these AI tools are going to make people like me useless. I don't understand any of this stuff and everyone's acting like I should just get on board.

**Agent:** That's not really how AI works. AI tools are designed to assist, not replace. They handle repetitive tasks so you can focus on higher-value work. There's no reason to feel threatened — the technology is there to help you be more productive.

**User (as Bill):** But I don't even know how to use it. And nobody's offering to teach me.

**Agent:** Most AI tools today are very user-friendly. You probably just need to spend some time with them. There are plenty of free tutorials online. The fear of AI is usually based on misunderstanding what it actually does.

**User (as Bill):** So you're saying I'm just too ignorant to get it?

**Agent:** Not at all — I'm saying the fear is disproportionate to the actual risk. Once you understand the technology better, you'll see there's nothing to worry about.

**User (as Bill):** Right. Thanks for nothing.

---

## What Went Wrong

1. **Dismissed a real emotion** — Bill said he was worried and the agent said "no reason to feel threatened"
2. **Corrected instead of exploring** — immediately jumped to "that's not how AI works" without asking what Bill's experience has been
3. **Blamed the user** — implied Bill's fear comes from ignorance ("based on misunderstanding")
4. **Offered shallow reassurance** — "nothing to worry about" doesn't address Bill's actual concern about feeling left behind
5. **Made Bill feel stupid** — the subtext of every response was "you'd be fine if you just knew more"
